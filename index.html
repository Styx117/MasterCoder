What does 'Master Coder' mean?

Through barely any extensive research, I have deduced that it can mean different things to different people...

I think that 'Master coder' has less to do with knowing the ins and outs of different languages, but being able to problem solve really well (i.e. researching a topic they're unfamiliar with, zeroing in on key aspects of a project when thinking about code architecture etc.)

- Gwaine

To me a master coder is somebody who has experience in programming and has the attitude of knowing how to create something while learning more.

- Ducky

I think a master coder is one who is not only proficient in programming but also problem solving in general.

- Styx

Let's look at the words 'Master' and 'Coder'...

Master (v.)

`Maistren, "to get the better of, prevail against; reduce to subjugation," from master (n.) and also from Old French maistriier, Medieval Latin magistrare. Meaning "acquire complete knowledge of, overcome the difficulties of, learn so as to be able to apply or use" is from 1740s. Related: Mastered; mastering.`

Code + er (coming from code) (n.)

(There are quite a few definitions so I went with this one) - "Any system or collection of rules and regulations"

So...

Master + Coder = "One who has acquired complete knowledge of any system or collection of rules and regulations" (So...Someone that knows 100% of any programming language?)

Since such a person doesn't exist, the next best definition is basically someone who's good at programming....Are you? Test your skills below (JS)

The Task


You've been given an array in the text editor. Your task is to make a function that counts how many times each word occurs in that array. You may not edit that array and to submit your answer, use the Console.log() command and the output will appear in the result section.

The desired output is this object: {cat:2,dog:1,cow:3}

Yes, I am aware you could just do console.log("{cat:2,dog:1,cow:3}");. But where's the fun in that?
